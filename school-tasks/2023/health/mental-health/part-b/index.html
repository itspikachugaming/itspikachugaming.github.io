Anxiety edition

Part B
Symptoms of anxiety
Anxiety is a mild feeling of worry. Some common symptoms of anxiety include nervousness, restlessness, feelings of danger, panic, rapid heart rate, rapid breathing, increased sweating, trembling and weakness.
Impact on health and well-being
Anxiety are emotions that can help us cope with challenges, but it can also have negative effects on our health and well-being if it becomes excessive. It can affect the body the nervous system, digestive system, immune system, respiratory system. low self-esteem, depression, social isolation, impaired functioning

 
Risk and protective factors of anxiety

These factors differ for each types of anxiety disorder but some common risk factors for anxiety include:

  • Stress during childhood and/or adulthood.
  • Shyness or other behavioral aspects in childhood
  • A history of anxiety conditions in relatives
How to cope with anxiety?
Some ways to cope with anxiety are:
  • Practice being focus and do deep breathing exercises. Try breathing in for 4 seconds then out for 4 seconds for 5 mins per day, this will calm you down.
  • Exercise. Get moving is the best way to stop having anxious thoughts. Slow moving workouts like yoga and walking is the best.
  • Journinaling. The 333 rule involves naming three things you saw during the day, three sounds you heard and touching three things, writing these down with your anxous thoughts may help you.
